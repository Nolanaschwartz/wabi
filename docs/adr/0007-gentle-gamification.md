# Gamification is gentle; care overrides engagement

Wabi's Streaks and XP exist to encourage healthy habits, but they deliberately reject the loss-aversion mechanics that standard gamification is built on. In a wellness context, shaming a struggling person for breaking a streak adds guilt to someone who came to Wabi *because* they're struggling — counter-therapeutic and against ADR-0001's non-clinical-care posture.

Rules:

- **Streaks celebrate, never shame.** A broken streak is framed with compassion ("welcome back"), never as failure. Optional grace / streak-freeze.
- **XP only accrues** — it never decays and is never taken away.
- **No dark patterns** — no guilt-tripping notifications, no loss-aversion pressure.
- **Care overrides engagement.** When a person's Mood or Tilt signals they're struggling, Wabi suppresses streak-maintenance nudges. When care and engagement conflict, care wins.

## Why

The default growth playbook reaches for loss-aversion streaks because they maximise engagement. Recording this decision stops a future PM from "improving" retention by reintroducing exactly the shame mechanics that would hurt the people Wabi exists to support.
