# LLM calls and Langfuse ingestion are shared kernels; fail policy and crisis redaction stay per-context

The mechanical half of every model call â€” resolve the provider, build the `@ai-sdk/openai` client, run `generateText`, normalise token usage, time the call, retry-on-empty â€” collapses into one deep module, `generate(role, opts)` in `@wabi/shared` (a new `./generate` subpath export). The six call sites that each re-implemented those steps (bot: `classifier`, `coach`, `intent-router`; research: `relevance-gate`, `extract`, `dedup`) shrink to *build prompt â†’ `generate` â†’ map result*. `generate` returns `{ text, usage, model, latencyMs }`, throws on transport error, and **does not decide what an empty or failed call means** â€” each caller keeps its own fail policy.

Separately, the content-agnostic Langfuse plumbing â€” `enabled` (lazy env), `shouldSample`, the batch-envelope POST with its in-flight set, and the shutdown flush â€” becomes a plain-TS `LangfuseIngest` kernel in `@wabi/shared` (`./langfuse` subpath). The bot's `LangfuseTracer` and a new research `ResearchTracer` are **two thin `@Injectable` adapters over that one kernel**, each owning its own trace shape.

## Why

- **The empty-output failure had no locality.** A reasoning model starved by too small a `maxOutputTokens` returns empty visible text and the call silently no-ops (it once dropped every paper against the local model, and once fired a crisis alert on every message). The guard against it was rewritten â€” or forgotten â€” at six sites with six different token-cap constants and only one (`coach`) actually retrying. One `generate` concentrates the diagnostic and the retry: fix once, fixed everywhere. Deletion test: delete `generate` and the five-step call boilerplate plus the empty-output handling reappear across all six callers â€” it earns its keep through locality, not substitutability.
- **`getProvider` must be resolved lazily (provider.ts), and `generate` makes that automatic.** Callers stop holding a `getProvider(role)` result in a constructor field; `generate` re-reads `process.env` on every call by construction, removing the load-order foot-gun rather than relying on each service to remember it.
- **Two tracer adapters justify the kernel seam.** The bot already traces; research now needs to. That is â‰Ą2 adapters over the ingest plumbing â€” a real seam, not a speculative one.

## Considered options

**Why `generate` owns the mechanism but *not* the fail policy.** The tempting move is a policy-carrying `generate(role, { onError, onEmpty })` that never throws and shrinks callers to one line. Rejected: the classifier's fail-to-`crisis` is the highest-stakes code in the repo (ADR-0021), and the gate/dedup fail-*open* and router fail-*soft* directions are genuinely different per call. Pushing those into a shared module moves safety meaning out of the Wellbeing context that owns it and flattens three deliberate, opposite directions into one configured value. `generate` owns the repeated *mechanism*; each caller owns the *meaning* of failure, visibly, beside the code that depends on it.

**Why the whole `LangfuseTracer` does not move to shared.** The intuition was to lift the existing tracer wholesale. Rejected: the tracer's crisis latch and content redaction are **ADR-0013/0024** safety mechanisms â€” a turn is latched crisis on its first crisis span and every later span is suppressed so crisis content can never leak. That belongs to Wellbeing, not a generic package; and research has no "turn" and no crisis concept (it traces runs and papers). Moving it would dilute a safety invariant into infra and force bot's coaching-turn span vocabulary (`classify | intent | coach | retrieval | memory`) onto research's traces. Only the content-agnostic ingest kernel is shared; trace shape and redaction stay per-context.

**Why `generate` does not own tracing.** A model call is a leaf; a trace is a tree of many calls under one `traceId`. `generate` has neither the `traceId` nor the `isCrisis` verdict (the classify call is *what determines* crisis), so a span emitted from inside it would route around the crisis latch. `generate` instead returns the span's leaf data (`text`, `usage`, `model`, `latencyMs`); the orchestrator â€” `coach-handler` for the bot, `research-agent` for research â€” owns the span tree and feeds it through its context's tracer.

## Consequences

- **`@wabi/shared` gains `ai` + `@ai-sdk/openai` as dependencies, isolated behind subpath exports** (`@wabi/shared/generate`, `@wabi/shared/langfuse`), following the existing `./sentry-scrub` precedent. The barrel `index.ts` does **not** re-export them, so `@wabi/web` â€” which imports shared for Prisma types and access resolvers and runs no inference â€” never pulls the inference libraries into its bundle graph.
- **The empty-output regression test moves to one `generate.spec.ts`** instead of living implicitly at six sites. Caller specs shed their `generateText`/`createOpenAI` mechanics and keep their domain assertions (classifierâ†’crisis mapping, router parse/validate, extract verbatim-substring check, dedup Jaccard), now mocking `@wabi/shared/generate`.
- **Sequencing is risk-ordered.** `generate` ships before the tracing kernel (the kernel work depends on `latencyMs` in `generate`'s return). Within `generate`, the research trio, `coach`, and `router` migrate first; the crisis `classifier` migrates last, behind its existing fail-to-crisis suite, because it sits on the safety hot path.
- **Research gains first-class tracing.** The `ResearchTracer` traces a run as a tree of per-paper `gate`/`extract`/`dedup` spans with no crisis latch â€” symmetric with the bot's turn tree, over the same ingest kernel.
- **`generate` retry-on-empty is opt-in.** `coach` opts in (a blank reply is worth one retry at lower temperature); the high-volume research triage calls opt out (fail-open is cheaper than a retry); the `classifier` opts out (it fails closed instantly â€” no added latency on the safety path).
